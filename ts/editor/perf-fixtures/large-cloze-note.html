First-Zero Extremal Duality at Support \Delta


At fixed Fourier support \Delta the one-level positivity argument has two ceilings — the min-side \beta_{\min}(\Delta) (forcing some \chi to have first scaled zero \le\beta) and the max-side \beta^\star_{\max}(\Delta) (forcing some \chi to be zero-free up to \beta). Both are now established sharp inside the method, by mirror-image extremal-function arguments. This page lays out the duality: what is common, how the optimisers coincide, and where the symmetry breaks.

The dictionary


The two ceilings are
\beta_{\min}(\Delta)=\tfrac{1}{2\Delta},\qquad \beta^\star_{\max}(\Delta)=\tfrac12-\tfrac{1}{2\Delta},\qquad \beta_{\min}(\Delta)+\beta^\star_{\max}(\Delta)=\tfrac12.At \Delta=2 both equal 1/4 — the joint 1/4-collapse that motivates the dissertation question. At any \Delta>2 the min ceiling drops and the max ceiling rises by the same amount \tfrac14-\tfrac{1}{2\Delta}, separating symmetrically about the collapse point \beta=\tfrac14 (where the count mean is \bar N(\beta)=2\beta=\tfrac12): min-side toward \bar N=0, max-side toward \bar N=1.

Common ancestor: the Beurling–Selberg interval problem


Both ceilings descend from the classical Beurling–Selberg interval extremal, which lets us replace the indicator 1_{[-\beta,\beta]} — the function we actually want to {{c1::integrate against the zero sum}} — by a band-limited proxy admissible in the explicit formula.

Count mean. Under GRH the one-level density at support \Delta asserts that, for any test f with \operatorname{supp}\widehat f\subseteq[-\Delta,\Delta] and unitary symmetry (W_{\mathrm U}\equiv 1),
\mathbb E_\chi\!\Big[\sum_{\gamma_\chi} f(\gamma_\chi)\Big]=\int_{\mathbb R} f+o(1).Setting f=1_{[-\beta,\beta]} would give t{{c2::he count mean, the family-averaged number of scaled zeros in [-\beta,\beta]}}:
\bar N(\beta):=\mathbb E_\chi[N(\beta)]={{c3::2\beta}}+o(1),with natural thresholds \bar N=0,\tfrac12,1 at \beta=0,\tfrac14,\tfrac12. The catch: the indicator is not band-limited, so it cannot be plugged in directly. Two equivalent ways to see it:

Direct (Fourier side). Compute
\widehat{1_{[-\beta,\beta]}}(\xi)={{c4::\int_{-\beta}^{\beta}e^{-2\pi i x\xi}\,dx}}=\frac{\sin(2\pi\beta\xi)}{\pi\xi}.This is a sinc — it {{c5::decays like 1/|\xi| and is nonzero on all of \mathbb{R}. "Band-limited at \Delta" means \widehat f vanishes outside [-\Delta,\Delta]; sinc never vanishes outside any bounded interval}}, so 1_{[-\beta,\beta]} fails the requirement for every \Delta.

Structural (Paley–Wiener). A function with \operatorname{supp}\widehat f\subseteq[-\Delta,\Delta] is {{c6::(a.e. equal to) the restriction of an entire function of exponential type \le 2\pi\Delta. Entire functions are continuous}}; the indicator has jump discontinuities at \pm\beta. Contradiction.

This is exactly why you need the Beurling–Selberg sandwich: you cannot feed 1_{[-\beta,\beta]} to the explicit formula (which demands \operatorname{supp}\widehat f\subseteq[-\Delta,\Delta]), so you replace it with band-limited M_\pm that bracket it, and pay the 1/\Delta excess as the price of band-limiting a discontinuity.

Minorant and majorant. Replace the indicator by entire band-limited functions sandwiching it. For exponential type 2\pi\Delta (equivalently \widehat{M_\pm} supported in [-\Delta,\Delta]) there are M_\pm with
M_-(x)\le 1_{[-\beta,\beta]}(x)\le M_+(x),\qquad \int M_\pm=2\beta\pm\tfrac{1}{\Delta},and the excesses \pm 1/\Delta are sharp as linear functional inequalities (Beurling 1930s; Selberg 1970s). Applying the one-level density to M_\pm pins the count mean from both sides:
2\beta-\tfrac{1}{\Delta}+o(1)\ \le\ \bar N(\beta)\ \le\ 2\beta+\tfrac{1}{\Delta}+o(1).
Beurling–Selberg sandwich. The majorant M_+ (blue) sits above 1_{[-\beta,\beta]} (black) with integral excess +1/\Delta (blue-shaded); the minorant M_- (red) sits below with integral deficit -1/\Delta (red-shaded). Both shaded areas shrink as \Delta grows — which is exactly what tightens the two ceilings.

The min and max ceilings are the two \beta-thresholds where this sandwich first crosses a meaningful constant:
  • Min side. If \beta>\tfrac{1}{2\Delta} {{c7::the minorant forces \bar N(\beta)>0, so some \chi has a zero in [-\beta,\beta]. Threshold \beta_{\min}(\Delta)=\tfrac{1}{2\Delta} (where the lower sandwich line 2\beta-1/\Delta first crosses 0)}}.
  • Max side. If \beta<\tfrac12-\tfrac{1}{2\Delta} {{c8::the majorant forces \bar N(\beta)<1, so some \chi has no zero in [-\beta,\beta]. Threshold \beta^\star_{\max}(\Delta)=\tfrac12-\tfrac{1}{2\Delta} (where the upper sandwich line 2\beta+1/\Delta first crosses 1)}}.

Count-mean sandwich. The shaded band is the rigorous bracket 2\beta-1/\Delta\le\bar N(\beta)\le 2\beta+1/\Delta around the true count mean \bar N=2\beta (black diagonal). The two ceilings appear as the unique \beta-values where the band edges first touch the horizontal thresholds: the lower edge crosses \bar N=0 at \beta_{\min}(\Delta)=\tfrac{1}{2\Delta} (red), the upper edge crosses \bar N=1 at \beta^\star_{\max}(\Delta)=\tfrac12-\tfrac1{2\Delta} (blue). Shown for \Delta=4 (1/\Delta=\tfrac14, \beta_{\min}=\tfrac18, \beta^\star_{\max}=\tfrac38); at \Delta=2 {{c9::both markers collapse onto \beta=\tfrac14}}, and {{c10::the band narrows}} as \Delta\to\infty.

The duality \beta_{\min}+\beta^\star_{\max}=\tfrac12 is literally the symmetry of the Beurling–Selberg excesses around the count mean \bar N=2\beta. The integer targets 0 and 1 on the \bar N-axis are not the operative thresholds: {{c11::to guarantee \bar N>0 you need the lower band edge 2\beta-1/\Delta\ge 0, and to guarantee \bar N<1 you need the upper edge 2\beta+1/\Delta\le 1. So the effective thresholds on the \bar N-axis are 0 and 1 each pushed inward by one band-limited approximation gap 1/\Delta, landing at \bar N=1/\Delta and \bar N=1-1/\Delta}}; read back through \bar N=2\beta these are {{c12::the two ceilings \beta_{\min}=1/(2\Delta) and \beta^\star_{\max}=1/2-1/(2\Delta), symmetric about \beta=1/4}}.
